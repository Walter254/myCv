    Personal Reflections On Polarization

    I find the topic of polarization particularly intriguing. One of the key insights I've gotten from talking to different people about this topic is the significant role that artificial intelligence and social media platforms, in general, play in exacerbating polarization. Algorithms that curate content based on users' preferences can inadvertently limit exposure to diverse perspectives, reinforcing existing viewpoints and deepening polarization. An aspect of polarization that continues to captivate my attention is its boundaries. Is polarization contingent upon a binary spectrum? Must there always be diametrically opposed viewpoints for polarization to manifest? Alternatively, can polarization exist independently of such a spectrum? Could it be that all individuals collectively contribute to polarization when they adhere to a singular truth? Furthermore, on an individual level, can individuals recognize their own polarization, or do they perceive their perspective as the sole truth, assuming unanimity among others? These questions arose from reflecting on my personal experiences, both within social media platforms and as an international student. During my time in Kenya, I staunchly aligned myself with a particular political party, believing it to be unequivocally right due to its ties to my ethnic background. However, exposure to diverse perspectives outside my community bubble, particularly upon coming to the US, led to a significant shift in my views. As I began to identify more with my nationality rather than solely my ethnic background, I realized the fallibility of my previous convictions. Engaging with individuals from various regions of Kenya, as well as broader discourse in the US, revealed starkly opposing viewpoints that challenged the foundation of my beliefs. This phenomenon mirrors the echo chambers often found on social media platforms, where users selectively engage with content and individuals that align with their ideologies. Such insular behavior can obscure the existence of dissenting viewpoints, hindering genuine understanding and empathy.
